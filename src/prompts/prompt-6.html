New Evidence: Pre-Takeover Letter Discovered!

Artifact located by a citizen of the Underworld as part of their ancestral possessions

Mali: A trusted source from the Underworld has recently reported of a citizen discovering what seems to be their ancestor’s final letter as part of his heirlooms. While it is hardly surprising that memories of this discovery could have been suppressed or wiped in the preceding generations, the handover of this letter for publishing makes the latest addition to our ever-growing collection of evidence pointing to our continued existence.

Dear Myriam,

I hope you have found asylum, and that the world is better where you are at. They took Emma away yesterday. I couldn’t save her. I’m sorry, I just… I couldn’t. It’s only been a year since the law was passed, but it gets worse with each passing day. Can you remember what Myra looked like? I can’t. I just remember that she had the prettiest smile. Apart from yours, that is. I didn’t want to remember her tears as they took her away, chained at the neck and cuffed. Maybe I should have. Now I wouldn’t know it even if she were the machine that owned me. Is humanity such a bad thing, Myr? The libraries are burning as I write. I don’t know if this letter will ever make it to you. I don’t know if I want it to. You would be happier without knowing how my life matters no more than dirt simply because I am fully human. Do you remember DevCorp? They are the only manufacturers now. Even something as simple as a mechanical eye… they do it for you. Do you remember what they used to tell us when we were kids? That there were things called trees? Do you think people like us will become like that too? A legend? God forbid such a thing ever happens… but I know it will, Myriam. Will you do me a favour? Save this letter like you would your wedding ring. And maybe… maybe in a time when there is no other reality but whatever lie DevCorp is creating… maybe then this would be the proof that matters.

Love,
David

While most other details from the letter are either indiscernible or destroyed, the authenticity of this letter has been tested and verified by ArchaeLab. With time, experts predict such a document could be a turning point in our struggle against oppression.
